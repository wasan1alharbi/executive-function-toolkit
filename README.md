# A Computer Science dissertation guide

This is the Dissertation branch of my executive-function-toolkit: the tools, resources, and small tricks that actually helped me get the project out of my head and onto the page. A CS dissertation can get complicated, and there isn't much online about how to actually do one. I put this together from my own experience, the mistakes I made, and what I'd do differently, so the next student doesn't have to repeat them.

If you're overwhelmed right now, that's normal and you'll be fine. Pick one section, do one small thing today. That's really all this is.

---

## How to use this guide

It's a living document, not a rulebook. I've seeded it with things I found useful, but it's most valuable once you make it yours. Keep what helps, ignore what doesn't, and if you've got something good to add, email me and I'll put it in.

The one thing I wish I'd known earlier: a dissertation is best done well from the first time. Even if the final writing is not complete, having all that is needed in one section is really helpful. This can work in small, repeatable chunks. Just aim to start. For example, if your task for the day is just "plan data gathering". Then write the entire outline you will need, just top level headings and think them through really well. This is one task mapped and became easy. Whereas, if you just put the raw data and called it a day, you risk more workload in another day. This is the simplest example I could think of. But please check what I have added already, and reach out or wait for more
